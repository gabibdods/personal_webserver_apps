Issue Solution
Understeer higher front wing, less front arb, front toe, bigger difference between front & rear ride-height, lower brake bias / off diff, higher engine braking
Oversteer higher rear wing, more front arb, smaller ride height gap, higher bbal + off diff, lower engine brake
Front locking lower brake bias, lower off diff, lower engine braking
Rear locking higher brake bias, higher off diff, lower engine braking
Overheating (f) more oversteer, lower bbal, higher engine brake
Overheating (r) more understeer, smoother driving, bbal more to front
Bottoming (f) higher front ride height, avoid kerb/bump
Bottoming (r) higher rear ride height, avoid kerb/bump
Spinning on Kerbs lift, wheels lose grip mounting and leaving zebras

What ERS modes should I run?

For qualifying, mostly medium and overtake. Hotlap for the long straights, like Monza or Vegas. Medium for the twisty sections, and overtake out of slow corners / short straights. For the race, don't be scared to turn the ERS off for some periods, as upshifting with no ERS will kick-start the recharge process. Be aware that turning the ERS off does cost you tons of speed.

What's up with the wings? Do they work?

F1 24 is completely different to previous games, and now we get rotation / downforce through the ride-height and rollbars more. For tracks such as Spain, Hungary, Qatar and Zandvoort, a standard 50-36 wing 21-21 rollbar combo will leave you in a bad place, as you're missing out on crucial rear downforce. This situation is that if you make one wrong move the car can just die on you, but with 5-21/10-21 arb the rotation comes much more naturally, and we are able to run much more rear wing without losing rotation. However, the extra rotation isn't for everyone - run a bit more roll-bar if struggling with oversteer.

Wet-Weather Changes

Wet weather in F1 24 is a bit different and the change list is smaller - you only need to turn down the on-diff to 85/90% and increase the front anti-roll bar + ride height by 2-3 clicks

F1 23 Setups

All setups are meant for online races but can also be used in career mode. Some tracks will be harder than others. Tracks in bold are notorious for having the AI use very low downforce levels that make them fast on the straights. Using lower wings than on the spreadsheet will come with a significant performance loss, especially on old tires.

Tire pressures are set in a way that tire wear is equal on all 4 tires unless the circuit is front / rear limited. Note that rears go off faster than the fronts, and tire pressures are symmetrical for the front and the rear. Do not try asymmetric tire pressures.
